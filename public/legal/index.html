AI Disclosure
AI Disclosure & Limitations
Effective: March 17, 2026 · Version 1.0

Important: PrimeCore Intelligence uses artificial intelligence to handle calls and assist agents. AI outputs may be incorrect, incomplete, or inappropriate for specific situations. Human oversight is required for high-stakes decisions.

What the AI Does

  • Processes spoken language and generates text/voice responses autonomously
  • Classifies caller intent and routes calls based on trained models
  • Provides real-time guidance to human agents via the Agent Assist overlay
  • Generates post-call summaries and analytics

Known Limitations

  • AI may misunderstand accents, dialects, or domain-specific terminology
  • Performance varies by language — Tier 1 languages (EN, ES, PT, FR) have lowest error rates
  • AI is not a licensed professional — outputs are never legal, medical, financial, or compliance advice
  • Context window limits may affect recall in very long calls (>15 minutes)

Prohibited Uses

  • Sole decision-maker for medical diagnoses or treatment recommendations
  • Sole decision-maker for legal advice or formal legal proceedings
  • Deceptive practices — callers must be informed they may be speaking with AI
  • Processing calls without appropriate two-party consent where required by law

Call Recording Consent

You are solely responsible for complying with call recording consent laws in your jurisdiction. In the United States, 12 states require two-party consent. In the EU, GDPR Article 6 applies. PrimeCore Intelligence’s auto-disclosure system fires a consent notice at call start — you must configure it correctly for your jurisdiction.
